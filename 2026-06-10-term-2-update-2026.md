## Wetland Eviction

Uganda's ongoing wetland restoration and eviction exercise has left many vulnerable families facing a humanitarian crisis. Many of the affected residents are ordinary citizens who invested their life savings in homes or small businesses. Today, some of these families are homeless, children have had their education disrupted, and many breadwinners have lost their means of survival.

## Ebola

Uganda continues to respond to an Ebola outbreak linked to cross-border transmission from the Democratic Republic of the Congo. As of 5 June 2026, Uganda had reported 19 confirmed cases and 2 deaths. Cases have primarily been reported in Kampala and neighbouring Wakiso District. Despite these efforts, affected households face significant challenges including loss of income, stigma, and disruptions to education.

## Rising Costs

Uganda is currently facing a rise in fuel prices due to global crude oil markets and regional tensions. This has greatly weakened the Uganda shilling against the US Dollar and increased transport and import costs, raising prices of essential goods and services and reducing household purchasing power — particularly affecting low-income families.
