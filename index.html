Who We Are

Quality education in the heart of a community that needs it most

Nakulabye Junior Primary School was founded in 1998 by Moses Zimbe, a local teacher who saw children around him — many orphaned by the HIV/AIDS crisis — going without education. He started with eleven children and a rented room.

Today, over 450 pupils study here. Many are orphans. Many have lost a parent. Nearly all come from families living in poverty. But every single one of them shows up, because this school gives them something the world around them doesn't always offer: hope and a future.

Our Story

From eleven children
to 450 — and growing

A school born from one man's response to a community in crisis. The story of Nakulabye Junior Primary School is one of courage, compassion, and the transformative power of education.

The Beginning

One teacher. Eleven children. A rented room.

In 1998, Moses Zimbe — a qualified teacher and local community leader in Nakulabye Parish — looked around his neighbourhood and saw a generation of children with no path forward. The HIV/AIDS epidemic was at its peak, leaving behind hundreds of orphans. Poverty was grinding and relentless. There was no community school, no government school, nowhere for the most vulnerable children to go.

So Moses built one himself. He gathered eleven children from his neighbourhood, mobilised the community to donate chairs and tables, and rented a space to start teaching. He taught most lessons himself. The school grew day by day.

That school became Nakulabye Junior Primary School — named after the slum community it serves, and committed ever since to the children that the rest of the world too often forgets.

Key Milestones

How the school grew

From a single rented room to a registered school with three buildings — the journey has been driven by the generosity of partners around the world.

1998

Founded

Moses Zimbe starts the school with 11 children in rented premises. He teaches most classes himself.

Early 2000s

Growing — but struggling

Pupil numbers grow rapidly. Wooden classrooms with no cement floors. Children arrive without books, pens, or pencils.

2004

First major partnership

ACET UK and ACET Czech Republic begin sponsoring children and donating materials.

2006

Rianna's Fund visits

A delegation visits the school and commits to funding new buildings on land ACET had purchased.

March 2008

New school opens

The new school opens on 7th March 2008 — six times the size of the rented premises, with a Junior School, Nursery, and kitchen/hall block.

Today

450+ pupils and growing

Registered with Uganda's Ministry of Education and Sports. 450+ pupils enrolled, 20% of whom are orphans.

"People used to laugh at us when we were studying in wooden classes. It has stopped them to laugh at us."
— Kasumba Regan, pupil aged 11, 2008
"Now I don't want to miss any day without coming to school. All my friends in the village want to come and join my school."
— Jala Isac, pupil aged 11, 2008

School Life

More than lessons.
A whole childhood.

At Nakulabye, education means building the whole child — through sports, the arts, debate, environmental action, and a community that celebrates every achievement.

The Curriculum

A strong academic foundation

Core subjects cover Mathematics, English Language, Integrated Science, Social Studies, and Religious Education. Lessons run approximately 105 minutes each and are designed to be interactive — using group discussions, storytelling, visual aids, and hands-on activities to suit every learning style.

In the nursery, children develop early literacy through phonics and letter formation, early numeracy, language development, and general knowledge — carefully designed to transition smoothly into the primary curriculum.

Arts

Music, Dance & Drama

Despite limited facilities, Nakulabye has consistently outperformed competitors in local and national MDD competitions. The school is proud to have produced national and international artists, musicians, and choreographers who have gone on to successful careers.

Students spend weeks preparing for competitions under dedicated teachers. The school's ambition: a music studio and theatre to give these extraordinary talents the stage they deserve.

Sports

Games & Physical Education

Football, netball, badminton, and tennis — the school has earned notable success in local and national competitions despite currently renting community grounds for games.

The school's next goal: a permanent multi-sport facility, allowing children to train properly and compete at the highest level.

Environment

Sustainability Training

Pupils learn about climate change through tree planting, recycling, and regenerative practices. The school is training a generation of environmental champions who will advocate for a healthier Kampala and a healthier planet.

Annual Event

Speech Day

Held at the end of every school year, Speech Day brings together students, families, teachers, and the wider community to witness performances in music, dance, drama, poetry, and public speaking. Academic and extracurricular achievements are celebrated with awards, and community leaders share messages of inspiration.

Community

Assemblies

Regular assemblies bring together all year groups — sharing news, celebrating achievements, and hearing from guest speakers. Students perform plays, recite poetry, and deliver speeches, building the confidence and self-belief that will serve them for life.

What you need to know

📅 Duration

Placements are flexible — from one week to several months. We'll work with your availability.

Volunteers arrange and fund their own accommodation. Kampala has a wide range of options — we can point you in the right direction.
